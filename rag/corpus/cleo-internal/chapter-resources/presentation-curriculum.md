Monthly Slides

### September: Introduction to Devices and Basic Navigation

Slideshow Title: Getting Comfortable with Your Devices

Show images of a smartphone, tablet, and computer.

Label the main components (power button, volume, home button, ports).

How to turn the device on/off.

Adjusting volume, brightness, and connecting to Wi-Fi.

Touchscreen gestures: Tap, swipe, pinch-to-zoom.

Basic mouse navigation: Pointing, clicking, right-clicking.

Adjusting font size and contrast for easier readability.

Changing wallpaper or background.

### October: Communication Tools

Slideshow Title: Staying Connected: Calls, Texts, and Video Chat

Step-by-step guide on how to make and receive calls.

Saving contacts and accessing them easily.

How to write and send texts.

Adding emojis and photos to texts.

How to join a Zoom or FaceTime call.

Adjusting audio/video settings during the call.

### November: Email and Internet Basics

Slideshow Title: Exploring the Internet and Email

Create an email account (Gmail or another provider).

How to compose, send, and read emails.

How to attach pictures or documents to an email.

Explain what an internet browser is (e.g., Chrome, Safari).

How to use Google to search for information.

Identifying safe websites (HTTPS, padlock symbol).

Avoiding suspicious links and pop-ups.

### January: Social Media and Staying Safe

Slideshow Title: Connecting with Family and Friends on Social Media

What is Social Media?

Brief overview of Facebook and Instagram.

Setting Up a Facebook Account

How to create an account.

Finding and adding friends/family.

Posting updates and photos.

Introduction to Instagram

Setting up an account and following friends.

Liking, commenting, and sharing posts.

Social Media Safety

How to adjust privacy settings.

Avoiding scams and fake accounts.

### February: Online Shopping and Digital Banking

Slideshow Title: Shopping and Banking Safely Online

Online Shopping Basics (Amazon)

How to browse, add items to cart, and place an order on Amazon.

Tracking deliveries and managing orders.

Introduction to Online Banking

How to log in to a bankâ€™s website or app.

Checking balances, viewing transactions, and transferring money.

Safety Tips for Online Shopping

Creating strong passwords.

Identifying secure websites (HTTPS, padlock).

Avoiding Online Scams

Recognizing phishing emails and fraudulent websites.

Tips for keeping personal information safe.

### March: Managing Photos and Cloud Storage

Slideshow Title: Capturing Memories: Photos and Cloud Storage

Taking and Sharing Photos

How to take pictures on smartphones or tablets.

How to share photos via text, email, or social media.

Introduction to Cloud Storage (Google Photos, iCloud)

What is cloud storage, and why is it useful?

How to upload and access photos in the cloud.

Organizing Your Photos

How to create photo albums and delete unwanted photos.

Managing Storage Space

Checking storage limits and freeing up space on devices.

Hands-On Practice

Seniors will take a picture, share it, and upload it to a cloud service (Google Photos or iCloud).

### April: Entertainment Apps and Streaming

Slideshow Title: Music, TV, and More: Using Entertainment Apps

Streaming Music (Spotify, Apple Music)

How to use music streaming apps.

Creating and listening to playlists.

Watching TV and Movies (Netflix, YouTube)

How to browse and watch content on Netflix or YouTube.

Searching for favorite shows or videos.

Using E-books and Audiobooks

How to read e-books (Kindle app) or listen to audiobooks (Audible).

Managing your book library.

Hands-On Practice

Seniors will explore a music or video streaming app and practice playing content.

### May: Digital Safety and Scam Awareness

Slideshow Title: Staying Safe in a Digital World

Password Safety

How to create strong, secure passwords.

Introduction to password managers.

Recognizing Scams and Fraud

Common scams targeting seniors (phishing, phone scams).

How to recognize fraudulent emails or websites.

Safe Internet Browsing

Staying safe online: avoiding risky websites, keeping software updated.

Hands-On Practice

Practice recognizing scam emails and setting up two-factor authentication.

### End of Year: Certificate of Completion and Celebration

In the final session of May, organize a small celebration to congratulate seniors for completing the year. Provide them with a Certificate of Completion for their efforts and encourage them to keep practicing their digital skills!

Quarterly Slides

### Quarter 1 (September-October): Devices, Communication, and Basic Internet Skills

Slideshow Title: Getting Started: Devices, Communication, and Basic Internet Skills

Objective: By the end, seniors will feel comfortable navigating their devices, making phone calls, sending texts, and using basic internet features like email and browsing.

#### Part 1: Introduction to Devices

Content:

Overview of smartphones, tablets, and computers (show images of each).

Basic device functions: power button, home button, volume control.

Navigating screens: touch gestures (tap, swipe), and mouse/keyboard usage.

#### Part 2: Communication Tools

Content:

Making and receiving phone calls.

Saving and organizing contacts.

Sending and receiving text messages (with photos and emojis).

#### Part 3: Introduction to Email

Content:

Setting up an email account (Gmail or Yahoo).

Sending, replying, and attaching photos/files in emails.

Email safety: avoiding spam and phishing emails.

#### Part 4: Basic Internet Browsing

Content:

Using internet browsers (Chrome, Safari).

Searching for information on Google.

Safe browsing tips: Recognizing secure websites (HTTPS) and avoiding suspicious links.

#### Hands-On Practice:

Help seniors navigate their devices, send a text message, send an email, and search the web.

### Quarter 2 (November): Social Media, Online Shopping, and Safety

Slideshow Title: Staying Connected: Social Media, Shopping, and Online Safety

Objective: By the end of this presentation, seniors will be familiar with using social media, shopping online, and staying safe in the digital world.

#### Part 1: Introduction to Social Media

Content:

Setting up a Facebook account.

Adding friends and posting updates/photos.

Adjusting privacy settings and recognizing fake profiles.

#### Part 2: Online Shopping Basics

Content:

Introduction to Amazon: Browsing, adding items to the cart, and placing an order.

Tracking orders and managing returns.

Safe shopping tips: Recognizing trusted websites and avoiding scams.

#### Part 3: Digital Safety and Scam Awareness

Content:

Creating strong passwords and using two-factor authentication.

Identifying and avoiding phishing emails, phone scams, and fraudulent websites.

What to do if you encounter a scam: Reporting and recovering from fraud.

#### Hands-On Practice:

Assist seniors in setting up a Facebook account, adjusting privacy settings, and practicing safe online shopping (simulated).

### Quarter 3 (January-February): Photos, Entertainment, and Cloud Storage

Slideshow Title: Enjoying Your Devices: Photos, Music, TV, and Cloud Storage

Objective: Seniors will learn how to take and organize photos, use entertainment apps (music and video streaming), and manage cloud storage.

#### Part 1: Taking and Sharing Photos

Content:

Taking photos and videos on smartphones/tablets.

Organizing photos into albums.

Sharing photos via text, email, or social media.

#### Part 2: Introduction to Cloud Storage

Content:

What is cloud storage? (Google Photos, iCloud).

How to upload, access, and share photos from the cloud.

Managing cloud storage space.

#### Part 3: Streaming Music and Videos

Content:

Using music streaming apps (Spotify, Apple Music): Searching for songs and creating playlists.

Watching videos on Netflix and YouTube: Browsing content, using subtitles, and adjusting video quality.

#### Part 4: E-Books and Audiobooks

Content:

Accessing e-books through Kindle.

Listening to audiobooks on Audible or similar apps.

#### Hands-On Practice:

Assist seniors in taking a photo, uploading it to the cloud, playing a song on Spotify, and finding a video on Netflix.

### Quarter 4 (March-May): Advanced Digital Skills and Review

Slideshow Title: Mastering Technology: Advanced Skills and Digital Safety

Objective: Seniors will deepen their understanding of online security, scam awareness, and device maintenance, while reviewing key concepts from previous quarters.

#### Part 1: Password Management and Security

Content:

How to create and manage secure passwords.

Setting up and using password managers.

Introduction to two-factor authentication for email, banking, and social media accounts.

#### Part 2: Avoiding Scams and Staying Safe Online

Content:

Recognizing phishing emails, phone scams, and fraudulent websites.

Reporting suspicious emails and blocking unwanted callers.

What to do if your account is compromised.

#### Part 3: Device Maintenance and Storage Management

Content:

Keeping devices secure: Installing updates, antivirus software.

Managing device storage: Deleting unused apps, clearing cache, freeing up space.

#### Part 4: Final Review of Key Skills

Content:

Review major skills: Email, social media, internet browsing, and cloud storage.

Recap on digital safety: How to stay safe online.

#### Hands-On Practice:

Have seniors practice changing their passwords to be different from eachother, recognizing scam emails, and managing their device storage.

### Final Celebration and Certificate Awarding (End of Year)

Celebrate the seniors' hard work with a small event where they receive a Certificate of Completion. Recognize their progress and encourage them to keep using and practicing their digital skills.
