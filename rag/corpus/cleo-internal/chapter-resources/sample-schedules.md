Sample Schedules

### Sample Schedule 1: Monthly Timeline

(Regular weekly tech support volunteering + monthly presentations and special events)

#### September: Introduction to Devices and Basic Navigation

Week 1:

Meet and Greet – Students and seniors meet for the first time to set expectations for the year. Introduction to CLEO, explaining regular volunteering and presentation structure.

Regular Volunteering – Students provide one-on-one tech support, assisting seniors with their devices.

Week 2:

Presentation – “Getting Comfortable with Your Devices” (Slideshow). Students give a structured lesson on basic device navigation, buttons, and settings.

Regular Volunteering – One-on-one tech support continues, helping seniors with specific questions related to their devices.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Students help seniors practice what they learned from the presentation.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Assist seniors with continued practice on their devices, reinforcing basic navigation skills.

#### October: Communication Tools

Week 1:

Presentation – “Staying Connected: Calls, Texts, and Video Chat” (Slideshow). Students present on making calls, sending texts, and setting up video calls.

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors practice using phones, texting, and video calling with their own devices.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continued one-on-one tech support, focusing on improving communication skills like texting and making calls.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Provide additional assistance with communication tools (texts, calls, video chats), helping seniors gain confidence.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Assist seniors with troubleshooting any remaining issues related to communication.

November: Email and Internet Basics

Week 1:

Presentation – “Exploring the Internet and Email” (Slideshow). Introduce email setup, sending emails, and safe web browsing.

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors set up an email account and practice browsing the internet.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Assist with sending emails, attaching files, and using web browsers safely.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors with continued email practice and navigating the web.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Focus on safe browsing habits, email etiquette, and troubleshooting internet issues.

#### December: Hot Chocolate Social

Week 1:

Regular Volunteering – Provide one-on-one tech support as usual, helping seniors with any unresolved issues from previous months.

Week 2:

Hot Chocolate Social – Fun, informal gathering for students and seniors to enjoy hot chocolate and socialize. No tech support this week.

Regular Volunteering – Briefly assist seniors before or after the social event.

Weeks 3 and 4:

Winter Break – No volunteering sessions.

January: Social Media and Staying Safe

Week 1:

Presentation – “Connecting with Family and Friends on Social Media” (Slideshow). Teach seniors about Facebook, Instagram, and social media privacy.

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors set up social media accounts and adjust privacy settings.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Assist seniors with navigating social media platforms, posting updates, and interacting safely online.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Continue social media assistance and focus on troubleshooting any privacy or account issues.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing tech support, helping seniors build confidence on social media.

#### February: Online Shopping and Digital Banking

Week 1:

Presentation – “Shopping and Banking Safely Online” (Slideshow). Teach online shopping basics, banking apps, and security tips.

Regular Volunteering – Assist seniors with setting up an Amazon account or online banking and provide safety tips.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors practice online shopping, reviewing order tracking and safe payment methods.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Provide support for online banking tasks (checking balances, transferring money) and reinforce online security.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Troubleshoot any shopping or banking issues, focusing on scam awareness.

#### March: Photos, Videos, and Cloud Storage

Week 1:

Presentation – “Capturing Memories: Photos and Cloud Storage” (Slideshow). Teach seniors how to take, share, and store photos.

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors practice taking photos and organizing them into albums.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Assist with cloud storage setup, uploading photos to Google Photos or iCloud.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Continue cloud storage support and troubleshoot any photo management issues.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing assistance with photo sharing and storage management.

#### April: Entertainment Apps and Streaming

Week 1:

Presentation – “Music, TV, and More: Using Entertainment Apps” (Slideshow). Introduce seniors to streaming apps like Spotify, Netflix, and YouTube.

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors set up and navigate entertainment apps on their devices.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continue assisting seniors with using music and video streaming services.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Provide ongoing support with entertainment apps, helping seniors discover new content.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Troubleshoot any remaining issues with apps and provide additional guidance.

#### May: Digital Safety and Scam Awareness

Week 1:

Presentation – “Staying Safe in a Digital World” (Slideshow). Teach seniors about password safety, recognizing scams, and using two-factor authentication.

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors implement safety measures like setting up strong passwords.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Practice recognizing phishing emails and scams with seniors.

Week 3:

Graduation Ceremony – Celebrate seniors’ completion of the CLEO program with certificates and a fun ceremony.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Final wrap-up session to answer any last questions and encourage seniors to continue practicing their skills.

### Sample Schedule 2: Quarterly Timeline

#### September: Devices, Communication, and Internet Basics

Week 1:

Meet and Greet – Introduction for students and seniors to set goals and expectations for the year.

Regular Volunteering – Assist seniors with any immediate questions or tech issues they may have.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continue providing one-on-one tech support, focusing on basic navigation and device setup.

Week 3:

Presentation – “Getting Started: Devices, Communication, and Basic Internet Skills” (Slideshow). Introduction to device functions, making calls, sending texts, using email, and browsing the web.

Hands-On Practice – Help seniors practice using their devices, making calls, sending emails, and searching the web.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing support for device navigation, communication tools, and internet browsing. Reinforce what seniors learned in the presentation.

#### October: Regular Volunteering and Tech Support

Week 1:

Regular Volunteering – Focus on addressing specific questions seniors have about communication tools and devices.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continue helping seniors with basic email use, browsing, and any issues they encountered with devices.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Provide troubleshooting for email issues, practice text messaging, and reinforce browsing safety.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing one-on-one assistance, helping seniors feel more confident in using their devices.

#### November: Social Media, Online Shopping, and Safety

Week 1:

Presentation – “Staying Connected: Social Media, Shopping, and Online Safety” (Slideshow). Introduction to Facebook, Instagram, online shopping (Amazon), and digital safety.

Hands-On Practice – Assist seniors in setting up social media accounts and walking through the basics of online shopping.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Provide additional support with social media accounts and help seniors adjust privacy settings or post photos.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Last session before winter break. Provide seniors with continued support and troubleshooting.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Continue providing tech support, with a focus on reinforcing social media and online shopping safety tips.

#### December: Regular Volunteering and Tech Support

Week 1:

Regular Volunteering – Continue to assist with social media, online shopping, and answer any pending tech questions.

Week 2:

Hot Chocolate Social – Informal gathering to enjoy hot chocolate and socialize.

Regular Volunteering – Briefly assist seniors before or after the social event.

Weeks 3 & 4:

Winter Break – No volunteering sessions.

#### January: Photos, Entertainment, and Cloud Storage

Week 1:

Presentation – “Enjoying Your Devices: Photos, Music, TV, and Cloud Storage” (Slideshow). Teach seniors how to take photos, use cloud storage, and navigate music/video streaming apps.

Hands-On Practice – Help seniors take photos, upload them to Google Photos or iCloud, and stream music or videos.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continue assisting with photo organization, cloud storage management, and using entertainment apps.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Focus on troubleshooting any issues seniors encounter with photos, cloud storage, or streaming apps.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Additional support for managing photos, discovering more entertainment apps, and troubleshooting any device-related problems.

#### February: Regular Volunteering and Tech Support

Week 1:

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors with any issues related to photos, cloud storage, or entertainment apps.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Focus on reinforcing the skills seniors learned in January, particularly photo storage and cloud management.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Continue one-on-one assistance, answering seniors' questions and troubleshooting issues.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing tech support to build seniors' confidence with their devices.

#### March: Advanced Digital Skills and Safety

Week 1:

Presentation – “Mastering Technology: Advanced Skills and Digital Safety” (Slideshow). Teach seniors about password management, two-factor authentication, and scam awareness.

Hands-On Practice – Help seniors set up strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and identify potential scams.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continue practicing security skills, focusing on scam recognition and email safety.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing tech support, helping seniors manage their passwords, security settings, and online privacy.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Address any remaining questions and reinforce online safety tips.

#### April: Regular Volunteering and Tech Support

Week 1:

Regular Volunteering – Provide support for advanced skills, such as setting up two-factor authentication or troubleshooting account security.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Continue assisting with online safety and helping seniors protect their digital accounts.

Week 3:

Regular Volunteering – Focus on troubleshooting any device issues seniors may be encountering.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Ongoing support for all learned skills, building confidence with device use.

#### May: Final Review and Graduation Ceremony

Week 1:

Regular Volunteering – Help seniors with any remaining tech issues or questions they have from earlier in the year.

Week 2:

Regular Volunteering – Reinforce all key skills learned throughout the year and troubleshoot any device or software issues.

Week 3:

Graduation Ceremony – Celebrate seniors' completion of the CLEO program with a formal ceremony, awarding certificates and recognizing their progress.

Week 4:

Regular Volunteering – Final session for wrap-up, answering any last-minute questions and providing encouragement for seniors to keep practicing their skills.
