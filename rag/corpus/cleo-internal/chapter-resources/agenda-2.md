# Agenda

> Source: CLEO Internal Resources - Chapter Resources/Event Resources/3. Lecture-Style/Agenda.docx

### Room Setup Guide

Seating Arrangement:

Arrange chairs in rows facing the presenter for clear visibility.

Provide extra space for wheelchairs or mobility devices if necessary.

Presenter Area:

Set up a podium or table at the front for the presenter.

Position the laptop, projector, or other presentation tools for easy access.

Technology:

Ensure the projector or large screen is connected and working.

Test the microphone or sound system for clarity.

Confirm the Wi-Fi connection is stable (if needed for the presentation).

Materials:

Place handouts or guides on a table near the entrance for attendees to pick up as they arrive.

Provide pens and paper for notes, if applicable.

Accessibility:

Make sure the room is well-lit and free of obstacles.

If virtual, test the platform and share the access link in advance.

### Agenda

Setup and Volunteer Briefing (15-20 minutes before start time)

Volunteers arrive early to help with room setup and test equipment.

Assign specific roles (e.g., greeter, tech support).

Welcome and Introductions (5-10 minutes)

Greet attendees and introduce the topic of the lecture.

Presentation (30-45 minutes)

Deliver the main content of the session.

Use clear, simple language and visual aids to enhance understanding.

Pause periodically to check comprehension and answer questions.

Interactive Activity or Q&A (10-15 minutes)

Provide an opportunity for seniors to ask questions or share their experiences.

Wrap-Up and Closing Remarks (5-10 minutes)

Summarize the key points covered in the lecture.

Share details about upcoming CLEO events or tech support sessions.

Encourage participants to practice at home
